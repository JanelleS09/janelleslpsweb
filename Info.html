SIPA

What is Sipa?

Sipa is a traditional Filipino sport and children's game that involves keeping a small object—usually a washer or rattan ball—aloft using only the feet. It's a test of agility, balance, and coordination, often played solo or in small groups. Sipa is commonly played in the streets, schoolyards, and during town fiestas as a cultural pastime.

How to Play Sipa

  1. Start Position: Stand upright and hold the sipa (usually a washer wrapped in string or plastic) in your hand.
  2. Release: Drop the sipa to the ground and prepare to kick as it bounces up.
  3. Kick: Use the inside of your foot to gently kick the sipa upward. Aim to kick under the center for the most lift.
  4. Repeat: Try to keep the sipa in the air as long as possible using controlled kicks.

History of Sipa

Sipa has been played in the Philippines for hundreds of years, with roots tracing back to pre-colonial times. It was originally played barefoot using a rattan ball or a metal washer wrapped in cloth or rubber. The game became a national symbol of Filipino creativity and simplicity.

Historically, Sipa was played in open spaces such as plazas or schoolyards. In 2009, Arnis was officially recognized as the national sport of the Philippines, but Sipa remains a beloved traditional game and cultural icon. It's a favorite among children and is featured in local sports festivals and celebrations.There are two popular varieties of Sipa; the first uses a lead washer, while the second uses a woven rattan ball. People often mistake Sipa for a children’s game because children prefer the washer in Sipa.
